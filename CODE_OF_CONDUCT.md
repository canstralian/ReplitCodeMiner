
# Code of Conduct

## üåü Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## üìã Our Standards

### Positive Behavior Examples
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Helping newcomers and sharing knowledge
- Celebrating diverse perspectives and backgrounds

### Unacceptable Behavior Examples
- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Spam or irrelevant content
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

## üéØ Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:
- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Code comments and documentation
- Community chat channels
- Social media mentions
- Events and meetups (virtual or in-person)
- Any other forums representing the project

## üõ° Enforcement

### Reporting
Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards. If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

- **Email**: Report to `conduct@your-domain.com`
- **GitHub**: Use the "Report content" feature
- **Direct Contact**: Message maintainers privately

### What to Include
When reporting, please provide:
- Your contact information
- Names of individuals involved
- Account of what happened
- Any supporting documentation (screenshots, links)
- Whether you'd prefer the report to remain confidential

### Response Process
All complaints will be:
1. **Acknowledged** within 24 hours
2. **Reviewed** by community leaders
3. **Investigated** fairly and promptly
4. **Responded to** with appropriate action

### Enforcement Actions
Community leaders may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

#### Warning
A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.

#### Temporary Ban
A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period.

#### Permanent Ban
A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## ü§ù Community Guidelines

### For Contributors
- **Be Patient**: Remember that everyone has different skill levels and experience
- **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Be Respectful**: Treat all community members with dignity
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together towards common goals

### For Maintainers
- **Be Fair**: Apply rules consistently and without bias
- **Be Transparent**: Explain decisions when appropriate
- **Be Responsive**: Acknowledge contributions and concerns promptly
- **Be Supportive**: Help community members grow and learn

### For Users
- **Search First**: Check existing issues before creating new ones
- **Be Specific**: Provide detailed information when reporting bugs
- **Be Grateful**: Acknowledge the work of volunteers
- **Be Patient**: Understand that responses may take time

## üå± Creating an Inclusive Environment

### Language Guidelines
- Use inclusive language that welcomes all backgrounds
- Avoid assumptions about technical knowledge
- Explain acronyms and technical terms
- Use "they/them" pronouns when unsure
- Choose clear, simple language when possible

### Cultural Sensitivity
- Respect different time zones and working schedules
- Be mindful of cultural differences in communication styles
- Avoid idioms or references that may not translate well
- Consider different perspectives and experiences

### Accessibility
- Use descriptive alt text for images
- Structure content with proper headings
- Ensure code examples are readable
- Consider users with different abilities
